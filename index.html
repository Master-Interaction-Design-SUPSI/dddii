Data Driven Design II

How can we visualise multiple types of data at the same time? What findings can multilayered information reveal about the urban landscape? To explore these questions and understand how different datasets can inform urban design, students worked in groups during a one-week workshop to investigate waste production data from the city of Lausanne. By creating visual prototypes, they explored ways to combine district-level waste information, demographic factors, and other relevant figures to identify patterns that might guide better resource allocation and highlight potential areas for improvement.

The workshop is part of the Data Driven Design II module at the Master of Interaction Design (MAInD), University of Applied Sciences and Arts of Southern Switzerland (SUPSI).

Teachers: Antonella Autuori, Ginevra Terenghi.
Teaching assistant: Luca Draisci.

17-21 February 2025.

The activity is part of the Blue City research project, led by Professor Jeffrey Huang at EPFL’s Media and Design Laboratory, and involving SUPSI, together with other partners (EPFL, ETH Zurich, and HES-SO) to understand how different datasets can inform urban design. By collecting and mapping data on waste, energy, water, and biodiversity, the team seeks to develop planning tools that can help decision-makers address key challenges in growing cities.

Tracing Glass

What does the journey of recycled glass tell us about Lausanne's waste management?

This visualisation maps glass collection routes, connecting waste production, vehicle movements, and efficiency.

Julie Agustoni, Valentina Guariglia, Francesco Salvatore, Luca Spallino

Waste Generations

Does age affect recycling habits in Lausanne?

This visualisation combines demographic data with recyclable and incinerable waste, looking for patterns and correlations in age-related sustainability information.

Tehreem Raheel, Lara Rodrigues Ristic, Nicolò Beghetto, Dajana Seefluth

Waste Wise

Does education influence recycling in Lausanne?

This visualisation links education levels with recycling efficiency and waste production trends, informing targeted sustainability strategies and effective policy-making.

Zheng Chen, Marco De Martino, Amaël Cacciola, Lolita Goncharova

Beyond Borders

How does nationality impact recycling in Lausanne?

This visualisation examines Swiss citizenship's influence on recycling behaviours, highlighting district-specific patterns and demographic factors.

Emma Baumhofer, Luca Ferrari, Felicia Ghercavii, Federico Emanuele Mameo, Antonia Siran

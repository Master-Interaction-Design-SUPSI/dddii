Data Driven Design II

In this workshop, participants studied waste production data from Lausanne to understand how different datasets can inform urban design. By creating visual prototypes, they explored ways to combine district-level waste information, demographic factors, and other relevant figures. The goal was to identify patterns that might guide better resource allocation or highlight potential areas for improvement. Through iterative testing and constructive feedback, the groups refined their data-driven interfaces, aiming to present complex information in a way that is both clear and practical for urban decision-makers.

The Blue City Project, led by Professor Jeffrey Huang at EPFL's Media and Design Laboratory, involves EPFL, ETH Zurich, HES-SO, SUPSI, and industry partners to examine urban flows in Lausanne. By collecting and mapping data on waste, energy, water, and biodiversity, the team seeks to develop planning tools that can help decision-makers address key challenges in growing cities. Their living lab approach emphasizes real-world testing and iterative improvements. Through these efforts, the project aims to propose informed, sustainable strategies that enhance urban well-being and resource management.

This workshop was held at SUPSI DACD Campus in Mendrisio, from Monday 17th to Friday 21st of February 2025, as part of the Data Driven Design II module, led by Antonella Autuori and Ginevra Terenghi.
